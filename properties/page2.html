The Soviet Union, officially the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR), was a country that existed from 1922 to 1991.

It emerged after the Russian Revolution of 1917, when the Bolsheviks overthrew the Russian Empire.

The USSR was a federation of multiple republics, the largest being Russia.

- Economy & Society: It followed a socialist/communist economic system, with state ownership of industry and agriculture.

- Collapse: Economic problems and political reforms under Gorbachev led to the breakup of the USSR in 1991, creating fifteen independent countries, including Russia, Ukraine, and the Baltic states.
